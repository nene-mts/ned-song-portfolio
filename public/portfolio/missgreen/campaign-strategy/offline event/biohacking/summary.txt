Biohacking Ventures

Launching a wellness sub-brand through events, operations, and bilingual event experience

Overview

Biohacking Ventures was a wellness-focused sub-brand developed under MissGreen, created to bring together health-conscious audiences through recurring offline talks, expert-led discussions, and community-based content. From the early planning stage, I was involved in building the project from the ground up — not only supporting event operations, but also shaping the brand itself.

My role spanned across brand creation, visual identity, event coordination, audience management, content setup, and on-site experience delivery. I was involved from the initial concepting stage through to monthly execution, helping turn the project into a repeatable event and community model that supported both brand building and MissGreen’s broader wellness ecosystem.

The Idea

The goal of Biohacking Ventures was to create a more engaging, trust-driven way to connect people with wellness topics beyond food alone. Instead of only marketing products, we created a platform where people could learn, ask questions, and experience the brand in a more human and community-led setting.

The format was designed as a monthly wellness talk series, with each event focused on a different health-related theme. This gave the sub-brand a clear rhythm, made content planning easier, and allowed us to steadily build a more recognizable offline presence.

At the same time, the project also supported MissGreen commercially and strategically. As a sub-brand within the MissGreen ecosystem, Biohacking Ventures helped strengthen credibility, deepen audience engagement, and create more touchpoints for long-term customer trust.

My Role

I was deeply involved across both the brand-building and event execution sides of the project.

On the brand side, I participated in the early development of the concept and was responsible for creating key parts of the visual identity, including the logo design and overall brand look and feel. I also helped establish its early communication presence across channels such as Xiaohongshu, Instagram, and WeChat content touchpoints, contributing to the sub-brand’s initial setup and rollout.

On the event side, I supported the full delivery cycle of each monthly session. This included:

* pre-event planning and preparation
* confirming venue setup and technical equipment
* organizing attendee registration and participant information
* coordinating speaker information and presentation materials
* on-site check-in, food distribution, and live event flow
* handling cross-functional coordination during the event itself

Because the events were held in China while operating primarily in English, I also helped create a more accessible bilingual experience for attendees through technical and operational problem-solving.

Bilingual Experience & Problem Solving

One of the most valuable parts of this project was building a practical bilingual event solution without relying on expensive external tools.

To support live understanding during English-based events, I explored and tested ways to provide real-time bilingual subtitle support. After researching paid tools such as iFlytek’s live interpretation products — which came with a relatively high annual cost — I developed a lower-cost alternative workflow using AI-generated subtitles, combined with available Apple audio tools and live display setup.

This helped us improve accessibility for Chinese-speaking attendees while also reducing operational costs. Beyond the technical outcome itself, it showed my ability to approach marketing and event work not only creatively, but also resourcefully — finding solutions that balanced audience experience, budget, and execution speed.

What I Delivered

This project resulted in more than just a series of events. It became an early-stage sub-brand system that included:

* a newly created brand identity and logo
* recurring monthly offline wellness talks
* speaker and audience coordination workflows
* bilingual accessibility support for live sessions
* social and content foundations for the brand’s early channels
* a stronger community-facing extension of MissGreen’s wellness positioning

In that sense, Biohacking Ventures functioned as both a brand-building project and an offline engagement campaign, with long-term value beyond each individual event.

Why It Worked

What made the project effective was the combination of brand clarity, recurring structure, and thoughtful execution.

The monthly format gave the brand consistency. The offline setting created stronger trust and intimacy than purely digital content. And because the events were supported by visual identity, social presence, and operational care, the experience felt more complete and intentional.

It also worked because the project created multiple layers of value at once: it gave audiences meaningful health conversations, gave the brand a distinct voice, and gave MissGreen a stronger community-based extension of its core business.

Reflection & Iteration

This project taught me a lot about how much event quality depends on the details behind the scenes.

After the first session, we identified a number of areas to improve, such as the visibility and positioning of speakers, the preparation level of presentation materials, audience flow, seating layout, and parts of the check-in experience. We also learned that while speakers all have different styles and cannot be fully standardized, stronger preparation and clearer briefing can still improve the overall audience experience.

Based on feedback from attendees and internal reflection after each event, we continued refining the process from session to session. That iterative improvement mindset became one of the most important parts of the project: not just delivering events, but learning how to make each one smoother, more professional, and more audience-friendly than the last.

Outcome

For me, Biohacking Ventures was one of the strongest examples of working across multiple layers of marketing at once: brand creation, operations, audience experience, content setup, and live execution.

It showed that I’m not only comfortable generating ideas, but also capable of building the systems, details, and coordination needed to make those ideas real — especially in early-stage, fast-moving environments where structure still has to be created from scratch.
